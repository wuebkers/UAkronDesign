"DESIGN IS MORE ABOUT OBSERVING THE WORLD AROUND YOU"

AJ Poholski

HONORS PROJECT

In recent years, the world has seen horrific environmental disasters causing irreparable damage in a very short amount of time. For my senior honors project, I have explored the impact that these environmental disasters have had on their surrounding environments, how they have affected both animals and humans and what prolonged effects they will continue to have.

The specific disasters I focused on are: The Exxon Valdez spill, The Baia Mare Cyanide spill, The Deepwater Horizon Oil spill, The radiation leak at Fukushima, and the Molasses spill in Honolulu Harbor. In each of these cases, there was an element of human error, such as a construction fault or an oversight which ultimately caused these events to occur or at least contribute in their severity. This human error was also an important part of my project.

I chose each of these disasters for a number of reasons. One reason is that these disasters are all relatively recent, the oldest being the Exxon Valdez spill which occurred 24 years ago. I also chose these disasters because of the variety of bodies of water affected by the spills. The Deepwater Horizon, Fukushima, and Exxon spills happened in the open ocean, the Honolulu Molasses spill occurred in a harbor, and the Baia Mare cyanide.

When considering the effects of these spills, the posters I have created depict the effect that these have on both the animals and humans in the area. I chose to focus on the effect that these spills have on both humans and animals in order to show how closely related we are and that when we harm the environment it effects us and everything around us in profound ways. These spills are not only killing mass quantities of wildlife but also decimating the industries that rely on this wildlife.
